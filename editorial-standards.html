Publication Policy

Editorial Standards

Neurology AI Pulse is a clinician-led monthly digest. Our goal is to provide distilled, high-signal insights that invite deeper reading — while maintaining a governance-first framing that prioritizes safety, privacy, accountability, and emerging cognitive rights.

Accuracy Transparency Clinical Relevance Governance-First No PHI Human-Led
Scope

What We Cover

  • Neurology-facing AI research and applied clinical workflows.
  • Generative AI in documentation, clinical decision support, and healthcare operations.
  • Governance and legal developments: privacy law, accountability frameworks, policy, and cognitive autonomy rights.
Sourcing

How We Select and Cite Materials

  • Primary preference: peer-reviewed journals, official guidance documents, reputable legal scholarship, and authoritative policy sources.
  • Each featured item links directly to the original source. Quotes are kept short and context-preserving.
  • We avoid sensationalism — selection is based on clinical relevance and governance significance, not novelty alone.
AI Use Policy

Use of AI Tools in Drafting

  • AI may assist with language tightening and structural clarity, but final editorial judgment remains human-led.
  • We reduce hallucination risk by anchoring all summaries to source content and linking directly to originals.
  • No patient-identifiable information (PHI) is published. Any clinical vignettes are de-identified and for educational purposes only.
Conflicts & Independence

Disclosure and Editorial Independence

  • No sponsorship influences content selection or editorial tone.
  • Any material relationships relevant to a specific item will be disclosed within that issue.
Corrections

Corrections and Updates

  • If a factual error is identified, we will correct it and note the correction in a subsequent issue.
  • Where possible, corrected language will also be updated in the hosted version of the affected issue.
Medical Disclaimer: Neurology AI Pulse is for educational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. All clinical decisions must be based on professional judgment and primary sources.
