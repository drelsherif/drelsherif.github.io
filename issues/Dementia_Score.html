Pre-Test Probability Calculator

This tool is designed for licensed healthcare professionals conducting cognitive evaluations in specialist or memory clinic settings.

The model estimates amyloid pathology probability using a Bayesian framework anchored to empirical amyloid PET prevalence data (Jansen et al. JAMA 2015, n=6,883) and adjusted by published odds ratios from the CAIDE validation cohort, APOE meta-analyses, and the 2024 Lancet Commission.

  • Not a diagnostic instrument — estimates pre-test probability only
  • Results must be interpreted within the full clinical context
  • Biomarker guidance aligns with the 2025 Alzheimer's Association CPG
  • Not validated for age <50, acute settings, or autosomal dominant AD

Prior AnchorAmyloid PET/CSF Prevalence Meta-analysis

Individual participant-level meta-analysis (n=6,883: 2,914 CN, 697 SCD, 3,972 MCI) establishing the baseline prior probabilities used in this model. Amyloid prevalence increases from age 50–90 in cognitively normal individuals: 10% → 44%. In MCI: 27% → 71%. APOE ε4 carriers have 2–3× higher prevalence at any age. This forms the empirical prior in our Bayesian framework — the single most important anchor in the model.

Jansen WJ et al. Prevalence of cerebral amyloid pathology in persons without dementia. JAMA. 2015;313(19):1924–38.

APOE ε4APOE Effect on ADRD — Largest Meta-analysis (Hispanic/Latinx, n=12,221)

Comprehensive 2025 meta-analysis providing adjusted odds ratios for APOE ε4 across ancestries. Joint analysis: ε4 heterozygous OR 2.30 (95% CI 2.07–2.56); ε4 homozygous OR 7.67 (95% CI 6.06–9.71). The ε2 allele is modestly protective (OR 0.81). Female ε4 carriers show consistently higher risk than male ε4 carriers, though the sex × APOE interaction used in this model is conservative (OR 1.26 additive interaction term).

Barral S et al. APOE and Alzheimer's disease and related dementias risk among 12,221 Hispanics/Latinos. Alzheimers Dement. 2025;21:e70138.

CAIDECAIDE Score — 19-Year Validation with APOE Integration

Validation of the CAIDE Dementia Risk Score in a national Finnish population-based cohort (n=5,806), with 571 dementia diagnoses over 19 years. AUC 0.78 (95% CI 0.76–0.79) for base CAIDE variables (age, education, sex, SBP, BMI, cholesterol, physical activity). Adding APOE ε4: AUC 0.81 (95% CI 0.80–0.83). The individual OR contributions from this study inform the vascular risk factor weights in this model.

Pietilä E et al. Enhancing dementia prediction: A 19-year validation of the CAIDE risk score with insulin resistance and APOE ε4 integration. J Prev Alzheimers Dis. 2025;12(5):100126.

AA CPG 2025Alzheimer's Association Blood-Based Biomarker Clinical Practice Guideline

Systematic review of 49 observational studies (n=560 mean, range 70–2244) assessing p-tau and Aβ assays. Key recommendations: BBMs with ≥90% sensitivity/≥75% specificity serve as triaging tests; ≥90% sensitivity and specificity serve as confirmatory tests substituting for amyloid PET or CSF. Pre-test probability must be considered — results should only be interpreted after comprehensive clinical evaluation. Preferred analytes: p-tau217, %p-tau217, p-tau181, p-tau231, Aβ42/40.

Palmqvist S, Whitson HE, Allen LA et al. Alzheimers Dement. 2025;21(7):e70535.

Lancet 2024Lancet Commission — 14 Modifiable Risk Factors

2024 update identifying 14 modifiable risk factors accounting for ~45% of global dementia. New additions: high LDL cholesterol (OR 1.42) and vision loss. Confirmed factors include hypertension (pooled OR 1.57), diabetes (OR 1.54), physical inactivity (OR 1.40), obesity (OR 1.40), depression (OR 1.90), low education (OR 1.60), and social isolation. These OR values are directly incorporated as model coefficients.

Livingston G, Huntley J, Liu KY et al. Dementia prevention, intervention, and care: 2024 report of the Lancet standing commission. Lancet. 2024;404(10452):572–628.

Therriault 2024Pre-Test Probability Framework for Plasma Biomarker Interpretation

Study of 6,896 individuals across 11 cohort studies (6 countries) establishing that PPV and NPV of plasma biomarkers vary substantially with pre-test probability. A 75-year-old with typical AD dementia syndrome: positive p-tau217 is almost certainly a true positive; negative result has significant false-negative probability. Validates the clinical necessity of pre-test probability estimation before biomarker ordering.

Therriault J et al. Diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease using plasma biomarkers adjusted to clinical probability. Nature Aging. 2024 Nov.

Jansen 2022Updated Amyloid Prevalence Estimates (85 cohorts, n=19,097)

Updated meta-analysis expanding the 2015 data. Among those without dementia: amyloid positive in 24% with normal cognition, 27% with SCD, and 51% with MCI. Amyloid prevalence consistently increases with age across all cognitive groups. Validates the age-stratified prior structure of this model and confirms the SCD prevalence (27%) as meaningfully above cognitively normal baseline (24%).

Jansen WJ et al. Prevalence of amyloid pathology in persons without dementia. Alzheimers Dement. 2022;18(6):1110–28.

MCI PrognosisMCI Conversion Rate Meta-analysis (89 Studies)

Systematic review and meta-analysis confirming that MCI conversion risk to dementia over mean 5.2-year follow-up is 41.5% in clinical settings and 27.0% in community samples. Amnestic MCI has higher progression rates than non-amnestic subtypes. Annual conversion rate approximately 8–10% in specialist settings (Mitchell & Shiri-Feshki meta-analysis: 9.6%/year). This contextualizes the syndrome stratum probabilities used in this model.

Salemme S et al. The prognosis of mild cognitive impairment. Alzheimers Dement Diagn Assess Dis Monit. 2025;17:e70074.

Model Architecture

This calculator implements a Bayesian logistic regression framework rather than a simple additive score. The fundamental departure from additive scoring tools is that variables do not contribute points — they contribute multiplicative odds ratio adjustments to a population-anchored prior probability.

The prior is set by the intersection of age × cognitive syndrome, using empirical amyloid PET/CSF prevalence data from the Jansen et al. JAMA 2015 meta-analysis (n=6,883). This prior is then adjusted multiplicatively by each clinical variable using its published odds ratio. The final posterior probability is derived via the logit-inverse transform.

Computational Steps

Step 1: Prior probability P₀ = f(age, syndrome) [Jansen et al. 2015 — empirical amyloid prevalence table] Step 2: Convert prior to log-odds logit(P₀) = ln(P₀ / (1 – P₀)) Step 3: Apply multiplicative OR adjustments logit(Pₙ) = logit(P₀) + Σ [ln(ORᵢ) × xᵢ] where xᵢ = 1 if variable present, 0 otherwise Step 4: Convert back to probability P_posterior = 1 / (1 + e^−logit(Pₙ)) Step 5: Confidence interval from data completeness CI_width = base_uncertainty × (1 / √data_weight) 95% CI = [P − 1.96×SE, P + 1.96×SE] SE derived from propagated OR 95% CI bounds

Confidence Interval Construction

The 95% credible interval is constructed via uncertainty propagation through the OR confidence intervals. Each variable's contribution to uncertainty is calculated as the half-width of its log-OR confidence interval divided by 1.96. These are combined in quadrature (assuming independence). The total SE in logit space is then transformed back to the probability scale using the delta method.

Variables not entered (marked unknown) contribute a fixed uncertainty penalty proportional to the range of plausible values for that variable type. Higher-weight variables (APOE, syndrome, age) have larger uncertainty penalties when missing, producing wider CIs and appropriate epistemic humility.

Depression as a confounder: When depression is present, an additional uncertainty term of ±8% is added to the CI because depression both mimics and independently causes cognitive decline, introducing genuine diagnostic ambiguity.

Limitations

  • ORs are assumed independent (no interaction terms computed), which overestimates certainty when multiple risk factors co-occur
  • Model has not been prospectively validated as a standalone clinical instrument
  • APOE ε4 effect size varies by ancestry — ORs from Barral et al. 2025 reflect Hispanic/Latinx and European ancestries; may overestimate in some other populations
  • Not applicable to autosomal dominant AD (PSEN1/2, APP mutations) — refer to genetic counseling
  • Amyloid PET prevalence data are primarily from research cohorts with volunteer bias; true community prevalence may be lower
  • MoCA modifies severity within syndrome stratum but has known floor/ceiling effects and education dependence
